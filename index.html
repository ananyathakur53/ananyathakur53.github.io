📊 Project: Indian Agricultural Production Analysis

Data Source: Government of India Open Data Portal (data.gov.in)

Dataset: Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare - State-wise Crop Production Statistics

Analysis Focus: Exploring patterns in wheat and rice production across Indian states using Python, Pandas, and Matplotlib

📍 Visualization 1: Scatter Plot

Rainfall vs Wheat Production in Indian States

Plot Type: Scatter Plot with annotations

Variables: X-axis: Annual Rainfall (mm), Y-axis: Wheat Production ('000 tonnes)

Sample Size: 10 major agricultural states

Key Observations:

  • States with moderate rainfall (600-900mm) show highest wheat production
  • Punjab and Haryana demonstrate optimal production despite lower rainfall due to irrigation infrastructure
  • Uttar Pradesh leads with 29,500 thousand tonnes at 850mm rainfall
  • Pearson correlation coefficient: 0.45 (moderate positive correlation)

Citation: Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare (2024). State-wise Crop Production Data. data.gov.in

🔗 Full plot available in GitHub repository: scatter_plot.png

📦 Visualization 2: Box Plot

Distribution of Crop Production in India

Plot Type: Notched Box Plot (comparative)

Variables: Wheat Production vs Rice Production distribution

Statistical Measures: Median, IQR, Outliers

Key Observations:

  • Wheat: Median = 11,500 thousand tonnes, IQR = 6,200-18,200
  • Rice: Median = 4,100 thousand tonnes, Higher variability with outliers (West Bengal: 15,500)
  • Rice production shows 65% higher coefficient of variation
  • Indicates specialized wheat belts vs diverse rice cultivation zones

Citation: Agricultural Statistics at a Glance (2024). Department of Agriculture, Government of India.

🔗 Full plot available in GitHub repository: box_plot.png

📊 Visualization 3: Bar Plot

Top 5 Agricultural States - Wheat vs Rice Production

Plot Type: Grouped Bar Chart with value labels

Comparison: Side-by-side wheat and rice production

States Analyzed: Top 5 producers by total output

Key Observations:

  • Uttar Pradesh: Total leader (43,700 thousand tonnes) - Wheat: 29,500 | Rice: 14,200
  • Punjab: Second highest (28,300) - Wheat-dominant due to Green Revolution
  • West Bengal: Rice specialist (16,450) - Highest rice production in India
  • These 5 states contribute ~62% of India's total food grain production

Citation: State-wise Agricultural Statistics (2023-24). Ministry of Agriculture, data.gov.in

🔗 Full plot available in GitHub repository: bar_plot.png

📈 Bonus Visualization: Line Plot

India's Production Trends (2018-2024)

Plot Type: Multi-line Time Series

Time Period: 7 years (2018-2024)

Metrics: Year-wise wheat and rice production trends

Key Observations:

  • Wheat: 16.2% growth (98,500 → 114,500 thousand tonnes) | CAGR: 2.55%
  • Rice: 20.0% growth (116,480 → 139,800 thousand tonnes) | CAGR: 3.13%
  • Both crops maintained growth during COVID-19, showing agricultural resilience
  • 2022 wheat dip due to March heat wave, recovered by 2024

Citation: Annual Report 2023-24. Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, Government of India.

🔗 Full plot available in GitHub repository: line_plot.png
